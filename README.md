## 📖 Project Overview

This repository contains my MSc Engineering Business Management dissertation completed at the **University of Warwick**.

The project investigates how **Big Data Analytics**, **Demand Forecasting**, and **SAP ERP (ABAP)** can be integrated to improve **last-mile delivery operations** within urban retail supply chains.

Using Python for data preparation and forecasting, and SAP ABAP for simulation, the project evaluates how delivery performance changes under different operational scenarios such as demand peaks and product returns.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

Urban last-mile delivery is one of the most expensive and challenging parts of the supply chain.

Logistics providers must continuously balance:

* Delivery time
* Transportation cost
* Vehicle utilisation
* Customer demand fluctuations
* Product returns

Traditional planning methods often struggle to respond quickly to changing demand, resulting in increased operational costs and reduced delivery efficiency.

This project demonstrates how data-driven forecasting combined with ERP-based simulation can support better logistics decision-making.

# ⚙️ Project Workflow

The project was completed in five major stages.

### 1. Research

* Conducted a Systematic Literature Review (PRISMA)
* Identified current challenges in last-mile delivery
* Reviewed ERP and logistics simulation approaches

---

### 2. Data Preparation

Retail supply chain data was:

* Cleaned
* Processed
* Analysed in Python

Outputs included:

* Demand forecasts
* Peak sales days
* Customer frequency analysis
* Return flags

---

### 3. Demand Forecasting

Python was used to:

* Build time-series forecasts
* Predict future order volumes
* Generate simulation-ready datasets

---

### 4. SAP ABAP Simulation

Forecast outputs were imported into SAP ABAP where custom business logic simulated:

* Delivery delays
* Peak demand scenarios
* Product returns
* Vehicle utilisation
* Delivery costs

---

### 5. Results & Business Insights

Simulation outputs were analysed to evaluate:

* Delivery performance
* Operational costs
* Vehicle requirements
* Logistics efficiency

The findings were translated into practical recommendations for ERP-enabled supply chain planning.
